PUSH-D-Delimited scope of use

The Information contained in, or accessed through, this platoform called PUSH-D is for your general information and self-help and self-development use only and is not intended to be used as medical advice and should not be used to diagnose, treat, cure or prevent any medical condition. The diagnosis and treatment of clinical depression requires a medical practitioner or qualified mental health practitioner. People seeking a diagnosis or treatment of depression, anxiety or other mental health disorders should consult a medical practitioner or mental health professional. This self-help platform is not designed to treat common mental health disorders. It is merely designed to help people to help themselves to cope better with depressive symptoms. In a general sense, people are likely to find the information on self-care to be useful; however this is NOT a substitute for seeking professional consultation and advice. There is no claim made that the material contained on this website is likely to be of help for everyone who uses this website. Privacy Policy

Personal Information

To enable you to engage with this self-help tool called PUSH-D, we will use personal information about yourself - that is provided directly to us during the registration process. Listed below are the information elements we collect during the registration process and how we use them:


  • Your email id: to verify your email for the registration process.
  • Your mobile number: to send you text-based prompts and reminders to work on your own well being goals.
    • Additional support for using this tool, for troubleshooting or resolving technical problems may also be provided via email/phone from time to time, as you desire and as may be feasible. However, provision of your phone number is not necessary for registration.
  • Your age, gender, occupation status, the city you live in, whether you have sought professional help for mental health earlier, and any other information that helps us to understand your needs and also the broad profile of the users of this tool.

When you use the PUSHD tool, we also collect personal information you provide in terms of your responses to the assessment questionnaires about your mood and mental well being, the feedback you provide to us, as well as the responses you enter when going over the app content and doing the self-help exercises to improve your well being. We also record the time at which you start and end sections and login sessions.


We may use your Personal Information to:

  • Assist you in working on your own goals of improving self care through reminders/clarifications as and when required
  • Gaining better understanding of your needs and offering you recommendations accordingly
  • Personalize your experience to the extent feasible
  • Conduct research in order to enhance scientific understanding and improving the utility of this self-help tool for future users (such research will ONLY use your information in an aggregate/summarized ( from multiple users) and anonymous way and cannot be linked back to you)
  • Detect and protect us against error, fraud, and other criminal activity
  • Make disclosures as may be required under applicable law

On the whole, when you register for and enter the PUSH-D Program, you will be asked to complete a number of questionnaires and enter some information, which you may consider to be sensitive, about yourself. The program asks for this information in order to provide you with recommendations that are personally relevant to you and help you learn skills to manage depressive symptoms. The information is stored in the PUSH-D database. This information may be reviewed by a Clinical Psychology consultant/staff who is part of the PUSH-D team at NIMHANS. This would be for the purpose of monitoring usage of the tool and progress of individual clients in order to guide them periodically, as may be required and feasible This information will also be used to periodically add/send notes from PUSHD-D consultants in the workbook of the users for clarification and motivation enhancement.

When you visit the platform, our servers automatically record information such as browser type, pages of our platform and sub-sections that you visit, the time spent on the site at each log in, and dates, and other general statistics. We use this information to analyze trends, administer the site, gather broad demographic information for aggregate use and improve this self-help platform by providing better user-experience.

All the information gathered is stored on servers managed by NIMHANS and is subject to stringent security measures.

Cookies

The website uses transient or session cookies. Cookies are pieces of information that a website transfers to your computer's hard disk when you open the program pages. Cookies by themselves do not identify you or any user but they do identify your session. Because this website only uses 'transient' cookies, when you log out or leave the website, the transient cookie set up by the website is destroyed, and no personal information is retained which could identify you or your browser next time you log on to the website. Please note that if your browser is set up to not accept cookies you will not be able to use all the functionalities of this platform.
